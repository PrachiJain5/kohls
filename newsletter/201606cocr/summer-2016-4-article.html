Saving for “the future”: What keeps you from actually doing it?

Probably because retirement might legitimately feel like an abstract event somewhere out in space (aka The Future). Then, out of the blue, you have one of those moments that reminds you time is zipping by. Like that time you got called “ma’am” or “sir” by someone younger than you. Or maybe when you stopped getting carded at movie theaters or bars.

Is the grey in my hair that obvious? Can I still pull off this outfit? Is my favorite song now a throwback? And it hits you: “One day” might be closer than you think.

Here’s the thing. While saving for the future might seem like something you can put off, a lot of people delay it because it can be intimidating. Here are three ways your Kohl’s benefits can help get you started, no matter where you’re starting from.

“I don’t even know where to begin.”

Who does? (Some people, fortunately.) Even better, many of them are happy to help those of us who don’t. Did you know you can get support from a new (and free) service called LearnVest? Talk to a real person about things like making a budget , saving for a trip or dealing with scary credit card debt (hint: they can help it become less scary). You can also use online tools to manage expenses, pay off loans and more.

Sign up now: Get started at learnvest.com/kohls.

“I signed up for LearnVest and am loving the program so far!”

—Kohl’s Associate

“Save for the future? I still have to pay my bills today.”

It can feel tough to put a bit of your paycheck toward your 401(k), especially if you don’t feel like you have much to spare. But here’s a reminder on “Company match” details to keep in mind: Whatever you shift from your paycheck to your 401(k)—let’s say $30 a month—Kohl’s will also add $30. Over one year, that’s an extra $360 in your savings. What you choose to contribute is totally up to you, and you can change it any time. Kohl’s will keep on matching your contribution, up to 5% of each paycheck.

And don’t forget about the power of compounding (aka “the earlier you start saving, the better off you’ll be”)! Even if you begin saving just a small amount, it will snowball—in a good way—much more than you might expect.

Now, great news for those of us who hate math! There’s an online tool to show you, in real numbers, how your savings can grow over the years. Log on to myHR.kohls.com to use the Real Future tool.

Full-time? Join the 401(k) whenever you’re ready. Kohl’s matches your contribution after your first year of service.

Part-time? Join the 401(k) (and get the Kohl’s match) after a year of service with at least 1,000 hours worked.
