You pay + Kohl’s pays = Self-funded

Self care. Self esteem. Self-motivated. Self assessment. You know those terms, but what about self-funded?

Self-funded is about health care and costs. When an employer pays for its employees’ health care benefits and services with its own money, that’s called being “self-funded.”

Kohl’s is self-funded. That means that Kohl’s covers the cost of the health care services you receive, not the health insurance company or some mysterious third-party organization. You also contribute toward the cost through your premiums, deductibles and coinsurance. But Kohl’s is ultimately responsible for funding the plan—and that’s an investment Kohl’s makes in you and your family.

Here’s how it works, in a super simple example:

Let’s say you’ve met your deductible. You see a doctor for a minor issue, and it costs $100 total. After your claim is processed, you get a bill for $20, and Kohl’s pays the rest—$80.

Managing costs is important, and it’s all tied to being a good health care consumer. It’s no secret that health care costs a lot, and it tends to increase over time. One way Kohl’s helps keep costs low is by offering best-in-class resources that help you get the right care at the right time and place. And, it’s also about making healthy choices. Smarter health care choices = lower costs for YOU and for Kohl’s.

Looking for ways to lower your own costs? Here are some to consider:

  • Be tobacco-free (or complete Kohl’s Tobacco Cessation Program) = Avoid the $1,800/year tobacco premium and save on cigs (at least $5 per pack these days!).
  • Try the Basic or Savers Plan = Lower costs per paycheck and more tax-savings with a Health Savings Account and in the Savers Plan, get $500 or $1,000 in your HSA from Kohl’s, depending on whom you cover).
  • Participate in Healthy Rewards = Earn points and prizes, and lower premiums (up to $1,200/year, if enrolled in a Kohl’s medical plan).
  • Know your spouse’s coverage options = If you choose not to cover a spouse who has other medical coverage available, you can avoid the $1,800/year working spouse premium.
  • Use Teladoc for health issues like ear infections and cold and flu symptoms = Less costly than a regular in-person doctor, urgent care or emergency room visit. Available 24/7 (nights and weekends).
  • Speak up with your doc = When your doctor recommends a test or writes a prescription, it’s ok to ask about price, location or alternative options. Be sure to check your preferred drug list and costs and get estimates on different treatments or procedures through Anthem , UnitedHealthcare or Kaiser.
  • Follow those preventive screening guidelines = Stay ahead of the curve with your health by having age- and gender-recommended screenings, like mammograms, colorectal screenings and immunizations. These screenings aren’t all that fun, but, they could save your life — like a mammography screening did for Kelly, a Kohl’s associate in Milwaukee.
  • Explore your options = With a little research—whether it’s online, through a second opinion or just a chat with your doc—you might find more cost-effective options.
