Think you understand health care?

3 ways to take control

Most people say they’re confident making health care decisions … until they need care! And unfortunately, that’s when many people stop trying to figure out what they should pay for medical services and just pay the bill when it comes.

When health care users stop trying to be effective consumers, they end up paying more than necessary. Would you pay $200 for something you could get for $150? Most people wouldn’t. Health care shouldn’t be any different.

So how can you take control—especially when you’re busy getting the care you need to get well? Take charge with these three steps:

Before you receive care

Brush up on the basics. Every person who has health coverage—whether through Kohl’s or another source—should know these five terms:

Your premium is what you pay from your paycheck for your medical coverage.

Your deductible is how much you pay each year before your insurance plan starts paying benefits.

After you meet the deductible, you and your Kohl’s medical plan share a percentage of the cost of medical services. This share is called coinsurance.

A copay is a flat dollar amount you pay for a specific service.

The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you’ll pay for your medical care in a year. Once you hit your out-of-pocket maximum, the plan pays 100% of your costs for the remainder of the calendar year.

Ready for more advanced learning? Visit justplainclear.com to brush up on other health care terms and concepts.

After you receive care

Carefully review the Explanation of Benefits Statement (EOB) you’ll get from your insurance carrier—Anthem, UnitedHealthcare or Kaiser, if you’re enrolled in a Kohl’s medical plan. This statement shows the services you received, the total cost of your care, the amount your medical plan covers and what you’ll need to pay. Be sure to wait for your health care provider’s bill, then compare it to your EOB before you pay your share.

  • If your insurance carrier pays less than you expected: Your first step? Call the member services number on the back of your medical ID card and speak with a representative. He or she can explain how the charge was processed and how benefits were calculated. Often, this one step can clear up questions you may have.

    Still disagree? Never fear. You’ll find instructions for appealing the carrier’s determination on your EOB. You can also find details in the Summary Plan Description (SPD) for your medical coverage (posted on myHR.kohls.com). It’s very important to follow the exact appeal procedures and deadlines that are described.

  • If you disagree with the charges from your doctor or other health care provider: You also have options. Read How to Review (and Dispute) Your Medical Bills for helpful tips.

  • If you’re not ready to navigate these issues on your own: Both Anthem and UHC offer advocacy services when you need help challenging EOB determinations and provider charges. Call the number on the back of your medical ID card to connect with trained professionals who are skilled at working with health care providers and insurance carriers to ensure the best possible outcome for you.

When you pay for care

If you’re enrolled in the Basic or Savers medical plan through Kohl’s, use your Health Savings Account (HSA) cash. The more you contribute, the more you save. Your contributions can lower your taxes, help you pay for out-of-pocket medical expenses and grow tax-free over time. Plus, having an HSA that you manage is another way to take control of your health care spending. The only catch is that to take advantage of an HSA, you need to be enrolled in either the Basic or Savers medical plan through Kohl’s (or another high-deductible health plan).

Not enrolled in one of these plans? Consider contributing to the Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA), so you can pay your eligible out-of-pocket medical costs with pre-tax dollars.
