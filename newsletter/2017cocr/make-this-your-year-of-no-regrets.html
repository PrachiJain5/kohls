Make this your year of no regrets

5 ways to avoid that “appointment-after” feeling

Regret. It’s that feeling of sadness or disappointment over something that’s already happened. Like missing your best friend’s wedding or forgetting a birthday or anniversary. Or even choosing a new home without doing your homework.

Ever had that feeling? You’re not alone. Especially when it comes to health care decisions. In fact, almost 80% of health care consumers recently surveyed said they’ve regretted a health care decision that led to getting the wrong care or care that cost more than necessary.* Most of the regret came from failing to check costs, jumping into treatment without asking the right questions or getting bad advice.

Not sure what to say or do?

Select an example below for inspiration.

  • Mary
    Mary’s doctor just prescribed a new medication.
  • Brad
    Brad hurt his knee playing basketball over the weekend. His doctor just recommended an elective surgery. He’s never had problems before.
  • Owen
    Owen’s doctor just prescribed an MRI.

Do you know what Mary should do?

Mary should...

  • Know these magic words when it comes to prescriptions:
    • Generic—a copy of a brand name drug. Generic drugs are required to have the same active ingredient, strength, dosage form and route of administration as the brand name.
    • Brand Formulary—brand name drugs that are included on the plan’s formulary list.
    • Brand Non-Formulary—brand name drugs that are not included on the plan’s formulary list.
    • Retail—medications obtained through a retail pharmacy (up to a 30-day supply)
    • Mail Order—medications obtained through the mail order pharmacy (up to a 90-day supply)

    To learn about other common terms, Mary can visit justplainclear.com.

  • Check the cost of her medication:
    • Basic Plan
    • Savers Plan
    • Exclusive Provider Plan
    • Kaiser Plan
  • Ask her doctor these questions:
    • What are the possible side effects? She should let her doctor know about any other medications or supplements she’s taking.
    • Are there more conservative treatments worth trying?
    • Are there less expensive medications that could work just as well?
  • Ask the same questions when she talks to her pharmacist.
    • She may learn even more about the medication, its side effects and possible alternatives.
    • Her pharmacist may also have info about special programs that can help with out-of-pocket costs.

Mary shouldn’t...

  • Fill the prescription without asking questions of her doctor or pharmacist.
  • Tell the pharmacist she doesn’t have any questions when he or she asks.
  • Ignore the recommended dosage.
  • Stop her course of treatment when she starts to feel better. She should check with her doctor first.

Do you know what Brad should do?

Brad should...

  • Ask his doctor if there are other treatments or therapies he could try before scheduling surgery.
    • For example, would it help to see a physical therapist?
    • Or would losing a few pounds help relieve pressure on his knee?
  • Get a second opinion to confirm that surgery is the right option.
  • If he concludes that surgery is the best course, research the cost of the procedure at different facilities as well as each facility’s success rate.

Brad shouldn’t...

  • Schedule the surgery, no questions asked.
  • Use the facility his doctor recommends without checking the cost or confirming that it’s in-network.

Do you know what Owen should do?

Owen should...

  • Research the cost of different MRI facilities in his area. There can be a huge difference in cost for this service, depending on whether he uses a hospital or other facility.
  • Confirm that the facility he’s selected is in-network.

Owen shouldn’t...

  • Schedule the MRI at the place his doctor recommends before checking the cost or confirming that the recommended facility is in-network.

Want to avoid that icky appointment-after feeling?

Here are 5 golden rules that will help.

1Use in-network docs!

Use the Anthem Anywhere or UHC Health4Me apps to find in-network doctors near home or work. Enrolled in the Kaiser Plan? Use “Find a Doctor” at kp.org. Special shout-out to Miss “I haven’t had a regular doctor since my mom stopped making my appointments”: Start by lining up an in-network primary care doctor who can get to know you, your family history and your needs.

2Get screenings and shots.

Schedule preventive care services and screenings as soon as they’re recommended. Yes, we’re talking to you, Mr. “I’m 55 and haven’t had a colonoscopy yet” AND Ms. “Close to 50 and haven’t gotten a mammogram.” What’s recommended depends on your age and health status. To help you keep track, Kohl’s has partnered with Evive to send you personalized reminders about your recommended screenings. We’re also providing on-site mammogram screenings at various Kohl’s locations this fall. Check myKohl’s for more information.

Enrolled in the Kohl’s medical plan? Get a free annual physical from an in-network provider for a snapshot of your current health. And here’s an added bonus: Get it done after July 1, 2017 and your lab results qualify for the Health Screening component of the 2018 Healthy Rewards medical premium credit. On-site screenings also will be offered in early 2018 at select work locations and through the Kohl’s Wellness Centers. Watch for more information.

Finally, don’t miss those important vaccinations to help you stay well. Like screenings, what’s recommended depends on your age and health status. Getting the flu is no joke, so plan on getting a flu shot this fall. We’ll offer free flu shots at select work locations, so check myKohl’s for more on the event dates and times near you.

3Don’t let little things turn into big things.

Ignoring seemingly minor things—like nagging knee pain or a bad cough—can lead to more serious issues. No pain, no gain? That’s flat out wrong, Ms. Weekend Warrior. If something hurts, get it checked out.

4Get the right care at the right place at the right time.

You have lots of options for getting health care. Choosing the right one for your situation is a great way to keep a handle on your out-of-pocket costs. Psst: Did you know that you—and your family members—can get most preventive services and treatment for a host of medical issues at our on-site wellness clinics? Check out the available locations and hours. See the Know Where to Go article in this issue for more tips on selecting the right care for your needs.

5Ask questions.

When it comes to your and your family’s health—and the health care you need—you’re in the driver’s seat. Yes, we’re conditioned to look to doctors and other health care providers as the experts—and they are. But that doesn’t mean you shouldn’t ask questions about your diagnosis and recommended treatments. It’s also a good idea to ask about alternative medicines and treatments and get second opinions.
