5 hidden gems of your health care coverage

You know those things you never think about, but you’re really glad are there when you need them?

Like car airbags or that spare set of keys in the junk drawer? Or how about when you’re traveling in a new place, and you stumble upon one of those little restaurants hidden on a quiet side street that ends up being your favorite part of the trip?

Some of the resources available with Kohl’s medical plan coverage are kind of like that: You might not know about them, or, you might think you’ll never need them. But as they say, life happens ... these terrific resources can be serious money- and time-savers.

1Teladoc

For when there are 101 things on your to-do list but no time for the doctor’s office, and the kids were up all night with ear infections ...

WHAT: 24/7 access—via phone!—to board-certified physicians for you and your family, for health issues like cold and flu symptoms, allergies, rashes and so much more.

HOW: Teladoc is part of the Anthem and UnitedHealthcare (UHC) medical plans (not Kaiser), and is like seeing a doc in person. Typically, Teladoc visits cost less than in-person appointments; the exact cost depends on your plan and the reason for your visit. Find out more at teladoc.com/kohls. There’s even an app.

PRO TIP: If you’re not enrolled in Kohl’s medical coverage, you can still enroll in Teladoc as a voluntary benefit for a monthly cost (check it out during this year’s enrollment). Visits are $45, versus an in-person visit, which is usually at least $100, depending on the type of doctor, where you live and other factors.

“This was a wonderful, easy, helpful experience.”

– Kohl’s Associate, on Teladoc

2Prescription drug coverage through CVS Caremark

Because even the healthiest folks need medicine from time to time!

WHAT: You might already know this one, but in case you don’t ... your drug coverage is bundled with your medical coverage. CVS Caremark also has a great app, medication reminders and a lower-cost mail-order option for certain drugs.

HOW: Visit caremark.com/kohls and download the app.

PRO TIP: You can sign up for automatic refills (huge time-saver!), find a local pharmacy, and learn about your meds all via the CVS Caremark website.

3“Your Nurse” from Anthem or UnitedHealthcare

For when a Google search just isn’t enough ...

WHAT: Access—for your whole family—to a specially trained nurse who provides extra-personalized help with your health care and medical concerns, on everything from post-hospitalization plans to chronic conditions to pregnancy. They can help answer questions, find providers, connect you to the right resources and more.

HOW: If you’re enrolled in an Anthem medical plan, call 877-529-1693 or visit anthem.com. If you’re enrolled in a UnitedHealthcare medical plan, call 866-204-6092 or visit myuhc.com. When you call, make sure to ask for “Your Nurse.”

PRO TIP: Have your member ID (found on your medical ID card) handy when you call.

“Very helpful and absolutely awesome to work with!”

– Kohl’s Associate on her experience with Anthem Health Guide (Your Nurse)

4Best Doctors

For when you get a diagnosis or treatment recommendation that makes you say, “Hmm...” or, “Do I understand all my options?”

WHAT: Get a second opinion for diagnoses and treatments, and find top-notch specialists. It’s free and confidential. Your expert guide can even talk to your other doctors if you’d like (with your consent).

HOW: Call 866-904-0910 or visit bestdoctors.com.

PRO TIP: You’ll get a full report on a USB flash drive you can reference.

“I think the level of report detail was great. I appreciate the recommendations and I feel good about my plan moving forward.”

– Kohl’s Associate, on Best Doctors

5Cleveland Clinic Travel Surgery Program

For when you or a covered dependent has a non-emergency, complex heart procedure.

WHAT: Best-in-class cardiac care from the world-renowned Cleveland Clinic—with medical and travel expenses paid for by Kohl’s. Note: there are certain clinical requirements for the program.

HOW: Call Health Design Plus (the administrator of the travel surgery program) at 877-230-0991, or visit clevelandclinic.org/kohls or kohls.hdplus.com. Have your associate ID handy.

PRO TIP: This is only available if you’re enrolled in the Kohl’s Basic, Savers (or Traditional Plan or Exclusive Provider Plan (EPP)).

In a bind with medical expenses?

Check out the Accident Protection Plan and Critical Illness Plan during the next Open Enrollment period. These voluntary benefits can be an “add on” to your Kohl’s medical coverage. They give you extra financial cushion that isn’t covered by your medical plan for certain serious illnesses or accidental injuries. For example, if you have a heart attack (and you’re enrolled in the Critical Illness Plan), the plan will pay a lump-sum amount to help you meet your deductible or everyday expenses like mortgage payments. Learn how they can help at myHR.kohls.com.
