Spend this, not that

The doctor’s bill arrives. Cue the internal panic. But wait: This time, it’s all good!

You know you’ve got it covered because you’ve been squirreling away money in a bunch of different places for occasions just like this. But do you know which account to reach for?

Thanks to the different types of accounts out there (yay, choices!), it’s harder than ever to know exactly which account to use (ugh, choices).

Quick quiz:

Q: If you have a Health Savings Account (HSA), the key word to remember is —?

A: Savings! You can use your funds for medical expenses now or later (btw, your money rolls over from year to year). And, by later we mean, a lot later ... like, even after you retire. Even better, if you sign up for the Saver’s Plan, Kohl’s contributes $500 to your HSA if you have single coverage, or $1,000 if you cover a spouse and/or dependents.

Q: If you have a Health Care Flexible Spending Account (HC FSA) the key word is —?

A: Spending! This money doesn’t carry over from year to year, so you gotta use it.

Good news! ALL of the accounts on this page use before-tax money.

Translation: The money comes out of your paycheck before you pay taxes. This is fabulous because: (1) It reduces the amount of money you get taxed on at the end of the year, which means you could pay less in taxes. (2) The money you take out of your account for eligible expenses isn’t taxed. (3) If you have the HSA, any interest your account earns isn’t taxed.
