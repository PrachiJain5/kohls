Impulse buys in the moment: Feels so good!

Later: Not so much...

There’s a thrill to impulse buying, isn’t there? The instant gratification is momentarily delightful.

But then, regret sinks in ... and not just because a snap decision ruined the budget—but because the seed of doubt takes hold. Could I have gotten a better deal?

When it comes to deals and dollars, you wouldn’t switch phone plans or buy a new car without shopping around, would you? Didn’t think so. News flash: your medical expenses deserve just as much thought as other important purchases. With medical expenses, the “impulse buy” often feels necessary, and sometimes it is—money is secondary when it comes to taking care of your family! BUT, in many situations, you have time to explore and find the option that’s right (and most cost-effective) for you.

Health care consumerism:

Considering different treatment options, asking your doctor questions, comparing costs, and thinking ahead about your health care decisions.

You’ve heard the phrase “good health care consumer,” right? It means doing a little research (like taking five minutes online to find an in-network doctor) so that you’re confident knowing you’re stretching your health care dollars and making the best decision for you (and your family). Bonus: Being a better consumer of health care can impact the amount you pay for your medical plan.

Sound a little intimidating? Being a good health care consumer isn’t as hard as you think. Here are four hacks to help you out.

Timing’s everything.

When you spend matters. If you need health care, will it be an ongoing expense, or a one-time thing? How does the purchase line up with when you get paid?

PRO TIP: Consider planning your preventive care visits (which are free to you!) early in the year, and anything else later, when you have more money in your Health Savings Account (HSA) (if enrolled in the Kohl’s Basic or Savers Plan).

When in doubt, map it out.

Before seeing your doctor, do a bit of online research (and no, Buzzfeed doesn’t count).

PRO TIP: Here’s a handy list of questions for your next visit. You can even use an app to locate and review nearby providers and facilities. Knowing this stuff before your appointment helps you plan and budget accordingly.

Don’t be afraid to say, “Hold on a minute...”

Let’s say your doctor gives you a prescription, or recommends a test. Don’t hesitate to ask about price, location or alternative options. With prescriptions, be sure you don’t run into any surprises at the pharmacy.

Pro tip: Check your preferred drug list and costs. Get cost estimates on different treatments or procedures through Anthem , UnitedHealthcare or Kaiser.

How’s your cash flow?

Before you swipe your card, think about which account makes the most sense to use. You can pay for medical expenses in different ways: credit cards, savings/spending accounts through Kohl’s, a checking account and good ol’ cash.

PRO TIP: If you have an HSA and can afford to let your funds accumulate (instead of dipping into them every time you’re at the doc or pharmacy), your account can grow over time—with interest.
