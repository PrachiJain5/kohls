Medical and Prescription Drug Coverage

You can choose from three medical plans—the Basic Plan, Savers Plan and Exclusive Provider Plan. The plans have many similar features. For example, they:

  • Cover the same services (like physician office visits, hospital and outpatient services and emergency room visits).

  • Use the same provider networks (Anthem or UnitedHealthcare, depending on where you work).

    UnitedHealthcare

    You’ll get the most value when you choose UnitedHealthcare network doctors. As long as you receive care from a UHC network provider, you’ll receive the higher in-network coinsurance level. However, you’re encouraged to seek care from UHC network providers that are recognized as top-rated for both quality and cost-effectiveness. These doctors are designated as “Tier 1,” so be sure to look for the UnitedHealthcare Premium Tier 1 symbol when you search for in-network providers.


    Anthem

    If you’re covered by Anthem, you’ll receive a higher benefit for using Anthem’s network of top-rated facilities for the following very specialized conditions: bariatric surgery, cardiac care, knee/hip replacement, spine surgery and transplants. Anthem designates these facilities as Anthem Blue Distinction Center + facilities.

  • Cover in-network preventive care (like annual physicals, well woman exams and checkups for your dependent children) at 100% with no deductible.
  • Provide financial protection in the event of a serious illness or injury.
