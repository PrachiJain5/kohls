Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs)

Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs) let you use tax-free money to pay for eligible health care and dependent care expenses.

  • Health Care FSA—If you enroll in the Exclusive Provider Plan or you have medical coverage through another source, you can contribute up to $2,600 tax-free to pay for medical, prescription, dental and vision out-of-pocket expenses for you and your dependents.

  • Dependent Care FSA (not for health care expenses)—You can contribute up to $5,000 tax-free to pay for child or dependent adult day care so that you (and your spouse, if married) are able to work or attend school.

    If you’re married, and want to participate in the Dependent Care FSA, your spouse must meet at least one of these conditions:

    • Work (either full-time or part-time)
    • Be actively looking for work
    • Be a full-time student
    • Be incapacitated

    • Note: The Dependent Care FSA cannot be used for dependent health care expenses, only for child or dependent adult day care expenses.

If you have an HSA, you are not eligible for the Health Care FSA; however, you can enroll in a limited-purpose FSA. It works like a regular FSA, but you can only use it for medical expenses after you’ve met the deductible. You can use it anytime for dental and vision expenses.

When Is a Limited-Purpose FSA the Right Move?

A limited-purpose FSA is the right solution if you want to use a pre-tax savings account to pay for a specific service—like a major dental procedure—early in the year, before you’ve contributed enough to your HSA to cover the full cost. That’s because with a limited-purpose FSA, your full annual contribution is available to you on January 1 of each year. But with an HSA, you need to have enough in your account to cover the cost of the expense to request reimbursement.

As you’re considering your options, keep in mind that limited-purpose FSAs are subject to the “use it or lose it rule,” which means you’ll forfeit unused funds at the end of each plan year.

The money in your FSAs does not carry over to the following plan year, so make sure to plan accordingly.

Manage Your FSA on the Go

If you enroll in an FSA, you can see balances, review alert/claims history, view payments and view and submit claims right from your smartphone. Download the Reimburse Me app available through Your Spending Account™ (YSA).

Note: FSA elections do not carry over from year to year. You must enroll when you are first eligible and during each Open Enrollment period if you want to use FSAs.
